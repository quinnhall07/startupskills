# One-Pager Structure

A single-page markdown or PDF document used for asynchronous communication — investor follow-ups, partnership intros, internal briefings. The one-pager replaces the pitch deck when (a) the audience won't sit through a presentation, (b) PMF is pre-signal and a polished deck would be premature, or (c) the founder needs something shareable in 2 minutes.

## The format

A single page. ~400–500 words. Sharp formatting. Top-to-bottom, no columns.

```
[Company Name] — One-line positioning

PROBLEM
2–3 sentences. Specific ICP, specific pain, specific cost-of-pain.
Customer language verbatim where possible.

SOLUTION
2–3 sentences. What you built (or are building). One sentence
on why this is non-obvious or hard to copy.

TRACTION
3–6 bullets. Numbers, customers, growth rate, retention. If
pre-signal, replace with "VALIDATION TO DATE": list the
behavioral evidence (Concierge MVP results, Fake Door
conversion, etc.).

ASK
What you want from the reader. Specific. Examples:
- "$1.5M seed, leading or co-investing."
- "Intro to 3 Heads of Procurement at Series B-C tech startups."
- "30-minute call to discuss whether a pilot is mutually
  interesting."

CONTACT
Founder name(s). Email. LinkedIn. Calendar link if appropriate.
```

## Section-by-section

### Title and positioning

The company name in bold. Underneath, a one-line positioning statement. Examples:
- "Carbon — Scope 3 emissions reporting for Series B-C tech."
- "PlanGrid — blueprints on iPads for construction sites."

NOT "The future of X." NOT "Reimagining Y." Direct and specific.

### Problem

The first paragraph should land the reader in the customer's situation. Use customer vocabulary from `references/research-playbook.md` and `market-intel`. Pull a verbatim quote if you have one strong enough.

NOT: "The world is changing." Specific or omit.

### Solution

What it is, in one breath. If you have a product, name it concretely ("a web app that pulls utility bills from your office leases and generates the SEC-format Scope 1-2 report"). If pre-product, describe the workflow you'll automate.

### Traction (or Validation)

If you have it: numbers, named customers (with permission), growth rate, retention, Sean Ellis score if ≥40%.

If pre-signal: rename the section "VALIDATION TO DATE" and list behavioral evidence. Examples:
- "Concierge MVP: 3 customers paid $500 each for 4 weeks of manual delivery (Mar–Apr 2026)."
- "Fake Door conversion: 8.4% across 1,200 ICP-targeted visitors."
- "Customer interviews: 24 ICP-targeted, mean evidence weight 0.6 (per Mom Test framework)."

The pre-signal one-pager is honest about being pre-signal. Investors prefer that to inflated claims.

### Ask

Specific. NOT "let me know what you think." Examples that work:
- "Seeking $1.5M seed. Lead or co-investing both welcome. Closing by July 2026."
- "Three intros to Heads of Sustainability at Series B-C tech startups."
- "30 minutes to discuss a 2-customer pilot at Acme."

A clear ask increases response rate by 2–3x over vague closes.

### Contact

Founder name(s). Email (use a real address, not a contact form). LinkedIn URL. Optionally a Calendly link, but only if the recipient is likely to self-serve.

## What never to include

- **A FAQ.** Save it for the call.
- **A roadmap with quarters.** Reads as fiction at one-pager stage.
- **Logos of customers you don't have.**
- **Logos of investors who haven't committed.**
- **A team section longer than two lines.** The one-pager is about the opportunity, not the team. Team goes in the deck.
- **Footnotes.** If a claim needs a footnote, put it in the deck.

## File format

- **Markdown** for sharing via Notion, Google Docs, or email body.
- **PDF** when going to investors who prefer attachments.
- **Plain text in email body** when the ask is small (a single intro). Inline beats attached for low-friction asks.

## When to use a one-pager vs a pitch deck

| Use one-pager | Use pitch deck |
|---|---|
| Pre-signal stage | Early-signal or above |
| Asynchronous outreach (cold investor email, partnership intro) | Scheduled presentation (investor meeting, demo day) |
| Recipient unlikely to sit through 12 slides | Recipient has committed 30+ minutes |
| You want to be in the conversation in 5 minutes | You want to control the narrative for 30 minutes |

Both can coexist. The one-pager opens the door; the deck walks through it.
