# External Resources

Curated canonical reading and watching list, organized by phase of the founder's journey. Skills surface the specific item at the moment it becomes useful — not as a dump at the start of the conversation. Every item has been vetted for substance.

## Discovery / idea generation

- **Paul Graham, *How to Get Startup Ideas*** (essay, paulgraham.com) — the canonical text on noticing ideas, the well metaphor, schlep blindness, and why "make something you want" beats "find a market."
- **Paul Graham, *Schlep Blindness*** (essay) — short and load-bearing. Why the boring, regulated, hard ideas are often the best ones.
- **YC Startup School video, *Where Do Great Startup Ideas Come From*** (Dalton Caldwell + Michael Seibel) — Dalton's distillation of how good ideas look at the moment they're had.
- **YC Startup School video, *How to Get and Evaluate Startup Ideas*** (Dalton Caldwell) — the 10-question framework that Startup Skills uses for `idea-pressure-test`.
- **Jared Friedman, *How to Get and Evaluate Startup Ideas*** (YC Startup School 2024) — the de facto 2024 successor to PG's 2012 essay. Pair with PG's original.
- **Dalton & Michael podcast** — "Where Do Great Startup Ideas Come From" (2024 episode): https://www.ycombinator.com/library/DU-dalton-michael-where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from
- **Lightcone Podcast** (Tan/Friedman/Hu/Taggar, 2024+) — "AI Startup Ideas," "Contrarian Bets": https://www.ycombinator.com/library/carousel/Lightcone%20Podcast
- **Tony Ulwick, *Jobs to Be Done: Theory to Practice*** (free PDF) — ODI, opportunity scoring formula: https://jobs-to-be-done-book.com/
- **Alan Klement, *When Coffee and Kale Compete*** — JTBD-as-progress: https://www.amazon.com/When-Coffee-Kale-Compete-products/dp/1534873066

## Founder profile / cofounder selection

- **Paul Graham, *How to Pick a Cofounder*** (essay) — short. Five qualities to look for. Re-read every six months while searching.
- **YC Startup School video, *How to Apply and Succeed at Y Combinator*** — team composition section is especially valuable for cofounder decisions.
- **YC Cofounder Matching** (startupschool.org/cofounder-matching) — free platform optimized for YC-shaped teams.

## Customer discovery

- **Rob Fitzpatrick, *The Mom Test*** (book, ~150 pages) — the canonical reading. Three rules: ask about their life not your idea; ask about the past not the future; talk less, listen more. The single most important book in the early-stage founder canon.
- **YC Startup School video, *How to Talk to User*** (Jared Friedman) — watch alongside reading *The Mom Test*. The interview shows the gap between "companies want to reduce emissions" and "the Head of Sustainability at Series B-C tech startups needs to report Scope 1-3 to their board, currently in Excel."
- **Steve Blank, *The Four Steps to the Epiphany*** (book) — Customer Development methodology. "No business plan survives first contact with customers." Customer Discovery → Customer Validation → Customer Creation → Company Building.
- **Teresa Torres, *Continuous Discovery Habits*** (2021) — the missing connective tissue. Opportunity Solution Trees, story-based interviewing, Product Trio, weekly cadence: https://www.producttalk.org/opportunity-solution-trees/
- **Bob Moesta, *Demand-Side Sales 101*** — Four Forces of Progress, the Switch Interview: https://www.amazon.com/Demand-Side-Sales-101-Customers-Progress/dp/1544509987
- **Bob Moesta on June.so** — full Switch Interview protocol walkthrough: https://www.june.so/blog/how-to-run-a-jtbd-interview-like-the-co-creator-of-the-framework
- **Indi Young, *Listening Deeply*** — phase-0 problem-space research, mental model diagrams: https://indiyoung.com/method/
- **Rob Fitzpatrick, *The Embedded Entrepreneur*** — audience-driven discovery, community-led validation: https://www.embeddedentrepreneur.com/

## Idea pressure-testing and the YC frame

- **Dalton Caldwell's *Tar Pit Ideas* talk** (YC video) — the canonical reference for the tar pit list.
- **Paul Graham, *How to Get Startup Ideas*** — the existing-competition section is the antidote to "no one is building this."

## MVP and rapid experiments

- **Eric Ries, *The Lean Startup*** (book) — Build-Measure-Learn, MVP as scientific experiment, innovation accounting. The conceptual frame for `rapid-experiments` and `mvp-architect` (both ship in v0.3).
- **YC Startup School video, *How to Build an MVP*** (Michael Seibel) — "Be desperate to leave features out." The brick metaphor (find a customer whose pain is acute enough they'll use a brick).
- **Paul Graham, *Do Things That Don't Scale*** (essay) — manual delivery, Collison installation, narrow first cohorts. Required reading for every founder.
- **Dropbox's video MVP** (search "Drew Houston Dropbox MVP video") — the canonical example of testing demand for a thing you haven't built yet.
- **Andrej Karpathy on vibe coding** (Feb 2025) — the framing: https://x.com/karpathy/status/1886192184808149383
- **Stack Overflow on AI tech debt** — 322% more privilege-escalation paths, 4x maintenance cost: https://stackoverflow.blog/2026/01/23/ai-can-10x-developers-in-creating-tech-debt/
- **MIT Sloan, Hidden Costs of Coding with Gen AI**: https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/the-hidden-costs-of-coding-with-generative-ai/

## First customers and sales

- **YC Startup School video, *How to Get Your First Customers*** (Gustav Alstromer) — covers the order of difficulty (personal network → startups → small companies → large companies), the manual sales motion, and why founders must do early sales themselves.
- **YC Startup School video, *The Best Way to Launch Your Startup*** (Kat Manalac) — the Continuous Launch strategy. Airbnb launched three times; Robinhood launched accidentally on HN.
- **Lenny Rachitsky's newsletter** (lennysnewsletter.com) — first-customer data sets, GTM motions by company type, pricing case studies. The single best source for empirically-grounded GTM analysis post-PMF.
- **Aaron Epstein, *How to Convert Customers with Cold Emails*** (YC Library): https://www.ycombinator.com/library/LZ-how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails
- **Pete Koomen, *Enterprise Sales for Founders*** (YC Library): https://www.ycombinator.com/library/LF-enterprise-sales-for-founders
- **Proofpoint on Feb 2024 Gmail/Yahoo bulk-sender rules** — required reading for any cold email work: https://www.proofpoint.com/us/blog/email-and-cloud-threats/clock-ticking-stricter-email-authentication-enforcements-google-start
- **Andrew Chen, "Revenge of the GPT Wrappers"** — defensibility in AI-era: https://andrewchen.substack.com/p/revenge-of-the-gpt-wrappers-defensibility

## PMF measurement

- **Rahul Vohra, *How Superhuman Built an Engine to Find Product/Market Fit*** (First Round Review article, ~30 minutes) — the operational blueprint. Sean Ellis survey, HXC segmentation, polarity analysis, fence-sitter conversion, 50/50 engineering split. Read this *before* you measure PMF, not after.
- **Sean Ellis's posts on the 40% Rule** — the leading-indicator survey. "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?"
- **Marc Andreessen, *The Only Thing That Matters*** (essay, 2007) — the qualitative complement to Sean Ellis. Servers melting, support overwhelmed, organic referrals. The lagging indicator.
- **Brian Balfour's essays on PMF** (brianbalfour.com) — channel-product fit, model-product fit. Reads PMF as a multi-equation, not a single threshold.
- **Casey Winters, "Guide to Finding PMF"** — retention curves as leading indicator: https://www.caseyaccidental.com/p/caseys-guide-to-finding-product-market-fit
- **Andrew Chen, "The Power User Curve"** — L7/L28 smile vs. frown: https://andrewchen.com/power-user-curve/
- **Reforge, "PMF Collapse"** — the PMF Treadmill: https://www.reforge.com/blog/product-market-fit-collapse
- **First Round Levels of PMF** — staging diagnoses with ARR/churn/NRR thresholds: https://www.firstround.com/levels
- **Todd Jackson / First Round, "A framework for finding PMF"** (Lenny's, 2024): https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/a-framework-for-finding-product-market

## Pivots

- **YC Startup School video, *All About Pivoting*** (Dalton Caldwell) — entire video is relevant. The opportunity cost frame, the difference between pivoting and chronic re-starting, what good pivots look like.
- **Eric Ries, *The Lean Startup*** — pivot chapter (zoom-in, zoom-out, customer-segment, customer-need, platform, business-architecture, value-capture, engine-of-growth, channel, technology pivots).
- **Annie Duke, *Quit*** — kill criteria, monkeys-vs-pedestals, "would you start this today?": https://www.annieduke.com/books/
- **Annie Duke on Lenny's Podcast** — better decisions: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/making-better-decisions-annie-duke

## General canon

- **Ben Horowitz, *The Hard Thing About Hard Things*** (book) — what to do when things break. Read after first hire.
- **Peter Thiel, *Zero to One*** (book) — contrarian on monopoly, secrets, definite optimism. Useful as a counterweight to YC orthodoxy.
- **Geoffrey Moore, *Crossing the Chasm*** (book) — the early-majority transition. Read once PMF is achieved and you're thinking about scaling channels.
- **Paul Graham, "Founder Mode"** (Sept 2024) — foundational for scaling: https://paulgraham.com/foundermode.html
- **Paul Graham, "Determination"** — the founder quality YC most weights: https://paulgraham.com/det.html
- **Garry Tan on The Knowledge Project (Ep. 226)** — earnestness, first-principles thinkers: https://fs.blog/knowledge-project-podcast/garry-tan/
- **Annie Duke, *Thinking in Bets*** — decisions vs. outcomes, resulting, calibration.
- **Phil Tetlock, *Superforecasting*** — calibration, Ten Commandments: https://www.amazon.com/Superforecasting-Science-Prediction-Philip-Tetlock/dp/0804136718
- **Kahneman/Sibony/Sunstein, *Noise*** (2021) — decision hygiene, MAP: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noise:_A_Flaw_in_Human_Judgment
- **Shane Parrish, Farnam Street decision journal**: https://fs.blog/decision-journal/

- **My First Million** — bootstrap and idea-stage discussions. Useful for `idea-genesis` pattern matching.
